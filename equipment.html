EQUIPMENT BREAKDOWN Deductible Limit

Covers damage to equipment by perils (accidents) that are excluded under the property wordings (i.e. explosion, overheating, cracking, electric arcing, power surge, mechanical breakdown, human failure). Requested limit must match the property limits above. Also covers resultant property damage and business interruption.

Include Consequential Loss (Spoilage) as a result of equipment breakdown Required Limit - $25000

CYBER/PRIVACY BREACH

What is Cyber/Privacy Breach? It is loss, theft, unauthorized access to, or accidental release of personal information. There are several ways a breach or attack can occur. Examples: Stolen or lost devices (ie laptops, discs, flash drives, etc.) or paper files, unauthorized access to or hacking of computer systems, employee error or oversight. Effective Nov 1/18 - The Digital Privacy Act (Bill S-4) and the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA) makes it mandatory for all organizations to report data breaches to affected individuals and the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada. Some Insurers can include this coverage as an extension under their commercial policies. If NOT available from the Insurer, then a stand alone policy can be quoted.
